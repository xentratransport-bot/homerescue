Emergency Plumber vs. Regular Plumber in NYC: When to Call Which

Not every plumbing problem is an emergency — but some absolutely are. Know the difference, know when to call 24/7, and know what NYC emergency plumbing actually costs.

Every NYC apartment owner or renter will face a plumbing decision at some point: call the emergency line (and pay the premium) or wait until morning. This guide tells you when each is the right call.

Always Call Emergency Plumbing (Don't Wait)

  • Burst or actively leaking pipe: Water damage compounds by the hour. A burst pipe that runs for 6 hours can cause 10x the damage of one caught immediately.
  • Sewage backup: Raw sewage in your apartment is a health emergency. It also indicates a main line blockage that will affect neighbors in shared-stack buildings.
  • Gas smell: This is not a plumber call — it's a Con Edison call (800-752-6633) and a building evacuation. Call Con Ed first, then emergency services.
  • No hot water in winter: A failed water heater in January with no backup is a legitimate emergency, especially for families with small children or elderly residents.
  • Flooding from any source: If water is entering the living space and you can't stop it by turning off supply valves.
  • Toilet backup with no other toilet: In a studio or 1BR with one bathroom, this qualifies as an emergency.

Can Wait Until Morning (Regular Appointment)

  • Slow drain (no backup, just slow)
  • Dripping faucet (put a towel under it and call tomorrow)
  • Running toilet
  • Low water pressure (not a sudden change — gradual issue)
  • Planned upgrades (water heater replacement before failure)
  • New fixture installation

NYC Emergency Plumbing Costs: What to Expect

Emergency plumbing in NYC is expensive — but knowing the range helps you avoid being overcharged.

  • Emergency service call (nights/weekends): $250–$500 just to show up
  • Burst pipe repair: $400–$1,500+ depending on location and access
  • Drain clearing (emergency): $300–$600
  • Sewage backup (main line): $500–$2,000+
  • Water heater replacement (emergency): $1,200–$3,500 installed

How to Minimize Emergency Plumbing Costs

  • Know where all your shutoff valves are before an emergency
  • Shut off the source immediately — every minute of running water is more damage
  • Get a quote before authorizing work — even in an emergency, any legitimate plumber will quote before starting
  • Call a company, not a solo Craigslist plumber — companies have insurance, licensing, and accountability
