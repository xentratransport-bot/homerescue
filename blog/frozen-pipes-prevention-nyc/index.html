Frozen Pipes in NYC: Prevention Guide for Apartments and Homes

NYC winters freeze pipes more than people expect. Prevention steps, what to do if a pipe freezes, and emergency response when it bursts.

Frozen pipes cause thousands of NYC water damage claims every winter. Unlike in truly cold climates where buildings are better insulated, many NYC apartments — especially pre-war buildings with exterior walls and uninsulated basement plumbing — are surprisingly vulnerable when temperatures drop below 20°F.

Which Pipes Are Most at Risk in NYC?

  • Pipes in exterior walls (common in pre-war NYC buildings)
  • Basement pipes in unheated or poorly insulated areas
  • Pipes in kitchen/bathroom cabinets on exterior walls
  • Hose bibs (exterior faucets) — should be winterized every fall
  • Vacant apartment pipes — no heat, no one to catch early signs
  • Uninsulated attic or crawl space pipes

Prevention Steps: Do These Before Cold Hits

  • Insulate exposed pipes in basements and exterior wall cavities with foam pipe insulation (hardware stores, under $5/linear foot)
  • Keep kitchen/bathroom cabinet doors open during extreme cold — lets warm air reach pipes in exterior-wall cabinets
  • Never let indoor temperature drop below 55°F — even when away or on vacation
  • Drip faucets slightly on pipes you're worried about — moving water resists freezing
  • Know your main shutoff — when a pipe bursts, every second counts. Know exactly where to go.
  • Drain hose bibs in fall — turn off the supply valve inside and open the outdoor faucet to drain remaining water

If a Pipe Freezes (But Hasn't Burst Yet)

  1. Turn off the water supply to that section if possible
  2. Open the faucet served by the frozen pipe — pressure relief when thawing
  3. Apply gentle heat: hair dryer, heating pad, or warm towels — never open flame
  4. Start nearest the faucet and work toward the frozen section
  5. Do NOT use high heat — rapid temperature change can crack pipes

If a Pipe Bursts

  1. Turn off the main water supply immediately
  2. Turn off electricity to the affected area if water is near outlets or panels
  3. Notify your building super (for co-ops/condos — they need to know)
  4. Call an emergency plumber
  5. Document everything with photos for insurance
  6. Begin water extraction immediately — mold starts within 24 hours
