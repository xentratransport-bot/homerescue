NYC HVAC Maintenance Guide: Keep Your System Running Year-Round

NYC HVAC tips for apartment owners and homeowners. What to do every season, when to call a pro, and how to avoid the most expensive HVAC failures.

New York City is brutal on HVAC systems. Summers hit 95°F with 70% humidity. Winters drop below 10°F with wind chills that stress boilers and heat pumps to their limits. A well-maintained system survives; a neglected one fails at the worst possible time — usually during a heat wave or cold snap when technicians are in highest demand.

Spring (April–May): AC Prep

  • Replace air filters (every 1–3 months depending on type)
  • Clean condenser coils on window units before installing
  • Test the system before first hot day — not during it
  • Check refrigerant — a unit that isn't cooling well may have a slow leak
  • Clear drain lines with a cup of diluted bleach to prevent summer mold
  • Schedule mini-split cleaning if you have a ductless system (annual is ideal)

Summer (June–August): Monitor and Maintain

  • Replace filters monthly during peak operation
  • Keep condensate drain clear — summer humidity creates blockages
  • Don't set thermostat below 68°F — systems ice up and fail
  • Check that outdoor condenser units have clear airflow — nothing within 2 feet

Fall (September–October): Heat Prep

  • Test heat before first cold night
  • Bleed radiators if you have steam or hot water heat (trapped air causes banging and cold radiators)
  • Schedule boiler inspection — required annually in NYC for building boilers
  • Replace furnace filters
  • Check thermostat batteries and calibration

Winter (November–March): Protect Against Freeze

  • Never let indoor temps drop below 55°F — pipe freezes happen fast
  • Insulate exposed pipes in unheated areas (basement, exterior walls)
  • Know where your main water shutoff is in case of freeze-burst
  • Keep cabinet doors under sinks open during extreme cold

When to Call a Pro

DIY maintenance goes this far. Call an HVAC professional when you have:

  • Unusual noises (grinding, banging, squealing)
  • Burning or musty smells
  • Ice forming on coils or refrigerant lines
  • System cycling on and off rapidly
  • Unexplained spikes in energy bills
  • Refrigerant leaks (illegal to DIY in NYC — must be EPA 608 certified)
