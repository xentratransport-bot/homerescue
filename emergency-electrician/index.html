Electrical Emergencies in NYC: Know the Warning Signs

New York City's housing stock includes buildings dating back to the 1880s, many of which still contain original or partially-updated electrical systems. Pre-war apartment buildings throughout Manhattan, Brooklyn, and the Bronx may have knob-and-tube wiring, outdated panel boxes, and aluminum branch circuit wiring that poses significant fire risks by modern safety standards.

Electrical fires are the leading cause of residential fire fatalities in New York. Many start inside walls or in electrical panels — invisible until it's too late. Knowing the warning signs of an electrical emergency can save your life and your home.

Warning Signs That Require an Emergency Electrician

  • Burning or melting smell: A burning plastic or electrical smell from walls, outlets, or your panel means wiring is overheating. This is a pre-fire condition — evacuate if the smell is strong and call both 911 and our emergency line.
  • Sparking or flickering: Sparks from outlets or switches indicate arcing — electricity jumping across a gap in damaged wiring. Flickering lights throughout a room (not just one bulb) suggest loose connections at the panel or in the circuit wiring.
  • Warm outlet plates or switch covers: Outlets and switches should never be warm to the touch. Heat indicates overloading or a wiring fault that's generating heat inside the wall.
  • Breakers that won't stay on: A breaker that trips immediately after reset indicates a serious fault. Do not force it — call our electricians.
  • Buzzing or humming from the panel: A properly functioning electrical panel is silent. Buzzing, humming, or crackling sounds from your panel indicate arcing, loose connections, or a failing breaker.
  • Multiple outlets dead on the same wall: Outlets on a shared circuit that all stop working suddenly may indicate a tripped GFCI, a blown fuse in older systems, or a wiring failure.

NYC-Specific Electrical Hazards

NYC's older building stock presents electrical hazards not commonly found in newer construction:

  • Knob-and-tube wiring: Found in NYC buildings constructed before the 1940s, knob-and-tube wiring lacks a ground conductor, cannot handle modern electrical loads, and deteriorates over decades. Most NYC insurers now require its removal before issuing homeowner's policies.
  • Federal Pacific Stab-Lok panels: Common in NYC buildings from the 1950s through 1980s, these panels have a well-documented history of breaker failure and are linked to thousands of residential fires nationwide. If your building has one, it should be replaced.
  • Aluminum branch circuit wiring: Used in some NYC construction during the 1960s and 70s, aluminum wiring requires special connectors and devices. Standard copper-rated outlets and switches are incompatible and can create fire hazards at connection points.
  • Overloaded circuits: Modern NYC apartments run significantly more electrical loads than they were designed for — large appliances, multiple air conditioners, EV chargers, and home offices can overload circuits in older buildings.
