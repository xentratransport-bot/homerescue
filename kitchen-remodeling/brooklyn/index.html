Brooklyn Kitchen Remodeling: The Borough's Character Demands Real Expertise

Brooklyn's kitchen renovation market is unlike any other borough. The housing stock spans 1880s brownstones in Park Slope and Carroll Gardens, early 20th-century rowhouses in Bed-Stuy and Crown Heights, post-war brick colonials in Bay Ridge and Dyker Heights, and modern glass-tower condos in Williamsburg and DUMBO. Each requires a fundamentally different approach — and a contractor who understands only one won't serve you well in another.

House Help Services has completed hundreds of Brooklyn kitchen renovations. We know the specific quirks of Prospect Heights pre-war plumbing, the structural considerations when opening brownstone walls in Carroll Gardens, and the condo board requirements in Williamsburg's newer buildings. Brooklyn homeowners are sophisticated buyers who invest heavily in their properties — our work reflects that expectation.

Common Brooklyn Kitchen Project Types

🏠 Brownstone / Rowhouse Open-Concept Conversion

Removing the wall between kitchen and dining room is the most requested Brooklyn project. Requires structural assessment; if load-bearing, a steel beam and temporary shoring are needed. Average cost: $45,000–$90,000 including structural work.

🍳 Galley Kitchen Modernization

Most pre-war Brooklyn apartments have narrow galley kitchens. We maximize storage and workflow within the existing footprint: floor-to-ceiling cabinets, built-in appliances, under-cabinet lighting, and smart organization. Average cost: $25,000–$55,000.

🏢 Condo / New Development Kitchen Upgrade

Williamsburg, DUMBO, and Downtown Brooklyn condos often have builder-grade kitchens. Upgrading countertops, cabinets, and appliances within the existing layout. Average cost: $20,000–$50,000.

🧱 Vintage Character Preservation

Many Brooklyn clients specifically want to preserve original tin ceilings, subway tile, farmhouse sinks, and hardwood floors while updating plumbing and appliances. We blend old and new seamlessly.
