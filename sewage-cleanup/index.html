Why Sewage Backups Are a Biohazard Emergency

Sewage contains Category 3 black water — the most dangerous classification in water damage restoration. Unlike clean water from a burst supply pipe, sewage carries:

  • Bacteria — E. coli, Salmonella, Shigella, Campylobacter, and more. Can cause severe gastrointestinal illness, kidney failure, and in vulnerable populations, death.
  • Viruses — Hepatitis A, norovirus, and rotavirus survive in sewage and can infect anyone who comes into contact with contaminated surfaces.
  • Parasites — Cryptosporidium and Giardia are common in sewage and resistant to standard household cleaning products.
  • Toxic gases — Methane, hydrogen sulfide, and ammonia accumulate in enclosed spaces during sewage backups, creating explosion and asphyxiation risks.

Do not enter a sewage-flooded area without proper protective equipment. Evacuate the affected space and call HomeRescue immediately at (212) 555-0199.

Common Causes of Sewage Backup in NYC

  • Clogged main sewer line — Grease, wipes, and debris accumulate in aging NYC sewer lines until flow is blocked entirely, causing backup into the lowest drains in the building.
  • Tree root intrusion — Tree roots infiltrate cracked sewer pipes and grow until flow is blocked. Common in Brooklyn, Queens, and Staten Island properties with mature trees.
  • Heavy rain and storm surges — NYC's combined sewer system can't handle extreme rainfall, causing sewage to back up into basements and ground-floor units during major storms.
  • Building sewer lateral failure — The pipe connecting your building to the city main can crack, collapse, or become misaligned — especially in older NYC buildings with clay or cast-iron laterals.
  • Flushed foreign objects — Wipes, hygiene products, and other non-flushable items create blockages that back up sewage into the property.
