Why Every Minute Counts with Water Damage

Water damage is the most time-sensitive home emergency. The longer water sits, the more devastating the damage becomes — and the more expensive the restoration.

Within the first 60 minutes, water begins soaking into drywall, carpeting, and wood framing. By 24 hours, mold spores activate and begin colonizing. By 48 hours, structural integrity can be compromised, and mold growth reaches levels that require professional remediation.

That's why House Help Services's 30-minute average response time isn't just a selling point — it directly reduces your total restoration cost by stopping damage at the earliest stage.

Common Causes of Water Damage in NYC

  • Burst or frozen pipes — the #1 cause of residential water damage in NYC, especially in older buildings during winter months.
  • Appliance failures — washing machines, dishwashers, water heaters, and refrigerator ice makers are common culprits.
  • Sewage backups — NYC's aging sewer infrastructure causes backups that require immediate extraction and sanitization.
  • Storm flooding — heavy rain, hurricanes, and nor'easters cause basement flooding across all five boroughs.
  • Roof leaks — damaged or aging roofs allow water intrusion that can go undetected for weeks.
  • HVAC condensation — AC units and heating systems produce condensation that can overflow and cause hidden damage.

Our Water Damage Restoration Process

Every House Help Services water damage job follows the IICRC S500 standard for professional water damage restoration:

  • Step 1: Emergency contact & dispatch — you call, we answer. A certified technician is dispatched immediately with all necessary equipment.
  • Step 2: Inspection & damage assessment — we use moisture meters, hygrometers, and thermal imaging to map the full extent of water intrusion. This documentation supports your insurance claim.
  • Step 3: Water extraction — industrial pumps and extractors remove standing water. For severe flooding, we deploy truck-mounted systems that extract thousands of gallons per hour.
  • Step 4: Drying & dehumidification — commercial air movers and dehumidifiers run continuously until moisture levels return to normal. We monitor daily.
  • Step 5: Cleaning & sanitization — antimicrobial treatments prevent mold and bacteria growth. Contents cleaning salvages your belongings.
  • Step 6: Restoration & reconstruction — we replace damaged drywall, flooring, paint, and trim. Your home returns to pre-damage condition.

Standard water damage restoration in NYC typically costs $1,000 to $5,000 for moderate incidents. Major flood events, sewage backups, or insurance-covered jobs can range from $5,000 to $20,000+. The cost depends on the area affected, type of water (clean, gray, or black), materials damaged, and whether mold remediation is needed. We provide a detailed upfront estimate before work begins.

Most homeowner's insurance policies cover sudden and accidental water damage — burst pipes, appliance failures, storm flooding. We work directly with your insurance company, provide complete documentation, and manage the claims process for you. Gradual damage (slow leaks you didn't address) and flood damage (requires separate FEMA flood insurance) may not be covered under standard policies.

Emergency water extraction takes 2–6 hours depending on volume. Structural drying typically takes 3–5 days with professional equipment running continuously. Full restoration including reconstruction can take 1–3 weeks for major damage. We set timelines during your initial assessment and keep you updated throughout.

Turn off the water source if possible. Switch off electricity in affected areas. Move valuables to dry areas. Do NOT use a regular vacuum on standing water.
